7.15: The Miscreants do not Surrender to Kåñëa

na māṁ duṣkṛtino mūḍhāḥ
prapadyante narādhamāḥ
māyayāpahṛta-jñānā
āsuraṁ bhāvam āśritāḥ

na—not; mām—unto Me; duṣkṛtinaḥ—miscreants; mūḍhāḥ—foolish; prapadyante—surrender; nara-adhamāḥ—lowest among mankind; māyayā—by the illusory energy; apahṛta—stolen; jñānāḥ—whose knowledge; āsuram—demonic; bhāvam—nature; āśritāḥ—accepting.

Those miscreants [duñkåtinaù] who are grossly foolish [müòhäù], lowest among mankind [narädhamäù], whose knowledge is stolen by illusion [mäyayä apahåta jïänäù], and who partake of the atheistic nature of demons [äsuram bhävam äçritäù] do not surrender unto Me [na mäm … prapadyante].













7.15: The Miscreants do not Surrender to Kåñëa

* adhamāḥ—lowest

… nara-adhamāḥ—lowest among mankind

* māyayā—by the illusory energy—the display of all material phenomena, of Kṛṣṇa’s opulence in the material world

* apahṛta—stolen

* asuram—demonic

… āsuram—of the demonic, to the demonic

* āśritāḥ—accepting, partaking; who have taken shelter of

* Those who are thoroughly befooled and bewildered by the illusory, material energy, by its influence through the body of the living entity, become miscreants who fail to surrender to Kṛṣṇa.

… The display of Kṛṣṇa’s material energy, its opulences, brings out the worst in them: their antagonism and belligerence towards Kṛṣṇa and so they do not surrender, na prapadyante.

* Such miscreants, duṣkṛtinaḥ, are four in number: 1. the grossly foolish, mūḍhāḥ 2. who are the lowest among mankind, narādhamāḥ 3. whose knowledge is stolen by illusion, māyayāpahṛta-jñānā 4. and who partake of the atheistic nature of demons, āsuraṁ bhāvam āśritāḥ
