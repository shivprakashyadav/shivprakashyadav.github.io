na—not; mäm—unto Me; duñkåtinaù—miscreants; müòhäù—foolish; prapadyante—surrender; narädhamäù—lowest among mankind; mäyayä—by the illusory energy; apahåta—stolen by illusion; jïänäù—knowledge; asuram—demonic; bhävam—nature; äçritäù—accepting

Those miscreants [duñkåtinaù] who are grossly foolish [müòhäù], lowest among mankind [narädhamäù], whose knowledge is stolen by illusion [mäyayä apahåta jïänäù], and who partake of the atheistic nature of demons [äsuram bhävam äçritäù] do not surrender unto Me [na mäm … prapadyante].

7.15: The Miscreants do not Surrender to Kåñëa

* adhamäù—lowest

… nara-adhamäù—lowest among mankind

* mäyayä—by the illusory energy—the display of all material phenomena, of Kåñëa’s opulence in the material world

* apahåta—stolen

* asuram—demonic

… äsuram—of the demonic, to the demonic

* äçritäù—accepting, partaking; who have taken shelter of

* Those who are thoroughly befooled and bewildered by the illusory, material energy, by its influence through the body of the living entity, become miscreants who fail to surrender to Kåñëa.

… The display of Kåñëa’s material energy, its opulences, brings out the worst in them: their antagonism and belligerence towards Kåñëa and so they do not surrender, na prapadyante.

* Such miscreants, duñkåtinaù, are four in number: 1. the grossly foolish, müòhäù 2. who are the lowest among mankind, narädhamäù 3. whose knowledge is stolen by illusion, mäyayäpahåta-jïänä 4. and who partake of the atheistic nature of demons, äsuraà bhävam äçritäù
